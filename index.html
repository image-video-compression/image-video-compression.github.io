Abstract

Deep learning has transformed image and video compression, shifting from hand-crafted transform coding pipelines to end-to-end neural networks that now surpass classical standards such as JPEG, JPEG 2000, and HEVC — yet existing surveys predate or only partially cover recent diffusion and state-space model literature.

This survey covers 200+ works (2010–2026) across three axes: (1) rate-distortion theory and the evolution of coding standards through VVC; (2) learned compression from early CNNs through GAN, Transformer, diffusion, and State-Space Model architectures; and (3) variable-rate and lossless coding with end-to-end loss functions. We report Kodak benchmarks, survey perceptual quality metrics, and discuss open challenges including neural codec standardization, foundation model-driven compression, and edge deployment.
